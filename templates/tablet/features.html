Features

Ubuntu offers an intuitive touch interface in tablet mode and all the power of a PC when in desktop mode.

  • Tablet turns into a full PC when connected to a wireless mouse and keyboard
  • Run two apps side by side for true on-screen multi-tasking when in tablet mode
  • Ubuntu’s unique scopes make content and services easier to access

Convergence that just clicks

With Ubuntu’s convergence features, you can now get a full PC experience on your Ubuntu Tablet. Simply connect a mouse and keyboard wirelessly to your tablet when it’s time to get something done, whether it’s serious work or serious gaming. And to enjoy your content on an even grander scale, you can connect to a monitor or TV. It’s the best of both worlds: portability and big-screen productivity.

The tablet that’s also a PC

Ubuntu Tablet lets you create and access documents, spreadsheets and presentations with LibreOffice, the Microsoft-compatible productivity suite. And when you need to see your documents at scale, just connect your Ubuntu Tablet to a monitor.

All your favourites on your Ubuntu Tablet

Whether you’re a gamer, a music maven or you’re glued to social media, there are loads of apps downloadable from the Ubuntu Store.

  • Firefox

    The speedy, independent, open source browser from Mozilla.

  • Dropbox

    The world’s favourite cloud backup and file sharing service.

  • LibreOffice

    The free office productivity suite that’s compatible with Microsoft Office.

  • Telegram

    The open source messaging service that uses your phone number as your ID.

  • Twitter

    The social media powerhouse that’s become an essential for online life.

  • Gimp

    The world’s number one free app for image creation and photo retouching.

  • Gmail

    The free and secure email service provided by Google.

  • YouTube

    The video-sharing app for viewing and sharing videos.

  • Soundcloud

    Listen to music, collect tracks, and follow friends for free, wherever you are.

Find things faster with scopes

Scopes give you access to content and services without having to download individual apps — so if you’re in a new city looking for things to do, a quick glance at the NearBy scope will give you an array of options ranging from restaurants to concerts to transportation. Whether you’re into music, interested in the latest news or looking for nearby dining options, Ubuntu scopes have you covered.
